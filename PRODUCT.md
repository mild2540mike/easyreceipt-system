# Product

## Register

product

## Users

EasyReceipt is used by Owners and Managers who oversee purchasing, branch budgets, stock levels, recipes, and member access, plus kitchen staff who need quick, low-friction workflows for recording purchases, checking ingredients, and reporting stock usage or stock-out events. Users often work in operational settings where they need clear numbers, fast entry, and confidence that stock and cost records are current.

## Product Purpose

EasyReceipt helps school or branch-based food operations control purchasing and inventory cost. It centralizes purchase recording, ingredient stock, recipe usage, stock alerts, daily branch budgets, reports, and member permissions so each branch can buy what it needs without losing cost visibility. Success means fewer stock surprises, faster purchase entry, clearer budget control, and reliable stock movement records.

## Brand Personality

Simple, professional, and easy to use. The interface should feel calm, practical, and trustworthy: a focused working tool that helps people finish tasks without needing training or interpretation.

## Anti-references

Do not make the UI feel complex, corporate-heavy, or overloaded with dashboard decoration. Avoid enterprise analytics aesthetics that prioritize charts and chrome over daily operations. Avoid dense admin clutter, excessive color, unnecessary cards, ornamental effects, and interactions that slow down kitchen or purchasing workflows.

## Design Principles

1. Put the daily task first: purchase entry, stock checks, recipe impact, and budget status should be direct and scannable.
2. Use calm operational clarity: typography, spacing, color, and labels should reduce hesitation, not add visual drama.
3. Make cost control visible at the moment of action: budget and stock consequences should appear before users commit changes.
4. Preserve role confidence: Owners, Managers, and staff should only see controls that match their responsibilities.
5. Prefer familiar product patterns: standard buttons, tables, modals, tabs, and form controls are strengths when they make the workflow faster.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to accessible product UI standards: readable Thai text, strong contrast for body and muted text, visible focus states, touch-friendly mobile controls, clear error messages, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Mobile layouts must be first-class because purchase and stock workflows may happen away from a desk.
